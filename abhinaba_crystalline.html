*Note*

Captain Manoj Kumar Pandey, PVC (Posthumous), was an Indian Army officer from Sitapur, UP, who fought in the 1999 Kargil War. He famously wrote in his diary, “Some goals are worth the failure and the fear.” He was killed while heroically capturing Khalubar Top. His final, defiant war-cry to his men was “Na Chhodu!” (“Don’t spare them!”), which this poem embraces as the powerful motto, “Never Quit.”
No silver spoon, no dynasty of war, Just Sitapur’s dust and a single star, A diary’s page, a promise crystal clear: “Some goals are worth the failure and the fear.” Burned mountains, a hell of ice and steel, His shoulder torn, his body forced to kneel, His glowing youth like a red stain, He rose again, defying all the pain. Blood mixed with snow, his vision growing dark, But in his chest still beat a burning spark. The weight of fallen brothers on his soul— Pushed him toward that final goal. He saw the flag, a final, fleeting sky, Drew one last breath, one last, ragged cry. It ripped the air; all his men did hear: “Na Chhodu”—Never Quit—loud and clear. I stood before his photograph today, Garlanded, silent, frozen in his fame. But silence cannot teach what words convey— The dead need living voices, not a frame. So let no marble bear his sanctified name, I’ll light a living, breathing, endless flame. I’ll find the kids in towns you’ve never known, The ones who fight their battles all alone. I’ll walk through lanes where hungry children play, Where fathers left and never found their way, I’ll teach them how to write their fear in ink, Then fold them into wings, watch them rise and think. Each dawn we’ll meet, we’ll say those words out loud, “Na Chhodu “—till broken becomes proud, Till every scar becomes a battle won, Till Manoj lives in each forgotten son.
